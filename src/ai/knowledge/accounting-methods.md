# Ogeemo Accounting Methodologies

## Cash vs. Accrual Accounting
Ogeemo provides flexibility in tracking finances but emphasizes understanding the "when" of recording income and expenses. The main difference between methodologies is all about timing.

### Cash Accounting
- **Focus:** Cash flow.
- **Definition:** You record transactions only when money physically changes hands.
- **How it works:** You log income when you receive payment, and expenses when you actually pay them.
- **Why it matters:** It accurately shows your immediate, physical cash flow, but not necessarily when you actually did the work. Ogeemo's BKS system simplifies bookkeeping by focusing on this cash basis.
- **Petty Cash Box Log:** Tracking physical cash in the business. The petty cash module tracks the physical float ("Cash In" and "Cash Out") before being "Posted to the GL" (General Ledger) for formal compliance.

### Accrual Accounting
- **Focus:** Profitability.
- **Definition:** You record transactions when they are earned or incurred, regardless of when money moves.
- **How it works:** You log income when you send an invoice (even if not paid yet), and expenses when you receive a bill.
- **Why it matters:** Accrual accounting provides a more accurate view of a company's financial health and profitability over a specific period, which is what banks and investors typically want to see. Ogeemo's Accounts Receivable and Accounts Payable modules are designed to help you track these accrual-based items.
